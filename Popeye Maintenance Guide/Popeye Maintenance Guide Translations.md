Although more than 20 years have passed since their Increasing!!!" The author, Kazuo Sakiyama, is a maintenance expert with a 60-year career. If you want to learn the secrets to keeping a car in healthy condition, he is the only person to ask!

Q1. What is the most important thing to do first to make a car last longer?


It is often said that modern cars don't break down,
while old cars are prone to breaking. But the truth is,
you have to accept that cars do break down. The model
year doesn't matter; there is no such thing as a
maintenance-free vehicle in the world. Also, I want to emphasize loudly that just because a car passes inspection doesn't mean it's fine! Vehicle inspections only confirm the temporary safety of the car on the day of the test; they do not guarantee safety for the following two years. If an accident occurs due to poor maintenance, it is not the inspector's fault but the owner's responsibility. That said, cars don't suddenly break down out of nowhere. If there is a problem, the car will always give signs through sounds or smells. To notice these signs, warming up the engine is very important. Many people start driving immediately after starting the engine, but that is not good. Listen carefully to the sounds during idling. Suddenly accelerating pots a strain on the engine, so the rule is to drive at about 30 km/h until around the second traffic light. Don't play music; listen to the car's voice and learn to recognize both when it is running perfectly and when it is not. For this reason, it is important for owners to constantly sharpen their five senses.

Even a car will feel unwell if it's suddenly woken up and told to drive. Remember that warming up is like warming up a car.

Q2. Are there any good things to do on a daily basis?

Make a habit of performing a 30-second visual inspection without feeling bothered. First, walk around the car and check the tires. How is the tread wear? Is the air pressure okay? Are there any cracks or scratches on the sidewalls? Then, once seated in the driver's seat, turn on the hazard lights and press the brake pedal several times to check if the blinking is functioning properly. Since it’s difficult to check all four corners alone, placing mirrors or reflectors in front of and behind the parking spot makes it easier to see.

When the tire tread depth falls below 1.6mm, the wear indicator, which shows the legal limit for use, will appear. Be careful of scratches and cracks on the sidewall, as these can cause a blowout. Both too much and too little tire pressure are bad. Know the optimal pressure level.

Q4. What should you do when you notice your car is not running well?


If any of the indicator lights lined up on the meter
panel are flashing, you must not ignore it. Those are
warning lights and carry important messages from the car. The urgency varies by color: yellow means caution and red indicates a serious problem that is critical. Therefore, the first thing is to understand the meaning of the warning lights. Also, pay close attention to any subtle feelings of "something is off" that only you can notice. It's helpful to note down on your smartphone the timeline and context of the issue, as well as any smells or sounds, so you can clearly communicate them to the mechanic.

It is ideal to prevent problems before warning lights appear. Therefore, you should develop the habit of checking the engine room. If you frequently check the engine oil level, the oil pressure warning light will not suddenly flash.

A strange smell is more serious than a strange noise. The level of danger varies depending on where the smell is coming from and what kind of smell it is. If the smell of gasoline fills the car, it could lead to a fire. Pull over and call a mechanic for their opinion.

Q5. How to take care of the car's body.


The cars I took care of commonly had mileage of 200, 000 km or 300,000 km. I wasn't doing anything particularly special, but I was thorough about two
things: using engine oil suitable for the engine and changing the oil frequently without skimping. Oil acts as the car's blood, providing lubrication, cooling, and cleaning functions, greatly affecting fuel efficiency and smooth acceleration. By checking the oil's dirtiness and level, you can also gauge the engine's health. There are many manufacturers and viscosity numbers like "15W-40" can be confusing at first, so how do you choose? Since it requires some knowledge, until you
 can judge for yourself, it's best to follow the advice your trusted maintenance shop.

I recommend "MOTUL". It has a low sulfur content, is less prone to oxidation, has a high cleaning effect, and makes the engine run smoothly. By the way, if the engine oil filter is dirty, the effectiveness of new oil will be halved, so you should change the filter at least once every two oil changes.

Q6. When is the right time to replace consumables.

They should be replaced sharply at about 80% before being completely used up. For example, brake pads. Some people ignore the squeaking noise, thinking, "The vehicle passed inspection, and there is still some pad left, so it should be fine," but that's never good. Aged brake pads are unusable, and the wear depends on how you press the brakes, so it's better not to rely too much on the replacement guidelines. The mindset of "it can still go" can sometimes be fatal. The timing belt, which is also the lifeline of the engine, is the same. Its average lifespan is said to be 10 years or 1 million kilometers, but this also varies depending on driving habits. Rubber and plastic consumable parts should be replaced decisively when the timing is right.

When brake pads exceed their limit, the metal plates rub against each other, making a grinding noise. Timing belts squeak. Make it a habit to note down the date you replaced consumables.

Q7. Is a cover necessary when parking in an uncovered parking lot?


Although body covers are said to be necessary for
keeping a car looking good for a long time, to be honest,
they are counterproductive. The reason is that sand and
dust blown in from the hem, combined with the cover fluttering in the wind, cause friction.

Q8. What kind of driving should you avoid to make your car last longer?

Needless to say, avoid sudden starts, rapid
acceleration, sudden braking, and sharp turns-any
driving with "sudden" actions. Therefore, the principles are "don't panic," "don't rush," and "be cautious and careful." Unless it's for racing, flooring the accelerator is out of the question. It's also unacceptable to speed on rough roads if the vehicle is not designed for off-road use. It is essential to drive appropriately, understanding the basic design and characteristics of the car. Also, some people shift from Drive mode to "N" or "P" at every traffic light, but it's best to stop doing that immediately. For automatic transmission vehicles, each time this is done, the clutch plates wear out, the AT fluid (lubricant) deteriorates, and nothing good comes of it
for the transmission. When stuck in traffic jams on the highway and unable to move for a while, it's safe to shift to "P," but normally, you should not change gears. Q9. After the winter ends, the area around the tires starts to rust...


Did you drive on snowy roads? The cause is salt
damage. You might think, "But I'm not near the sea?" However, the main ingredient of the de-icing agents spread on roads to melt snow is calcium chloride. If it
gets kicked up by the tires and stays there, it causes rust due to salt damage. Therefore, if you drive on snowy roads, wash the undercarriage with fresh water of the same day. Use a hose to thoroughly clean every detail.

No matter how cold it is, wash your car immediately after driving on snowy roads. Spray water on the tires and the entire undercarriage from various angles.
Wash it off in the same way if you've been exposed to the sea breeze for a long time.

Q10. Keeping the interior clean.


If you don't clean the inside of the car, bacteria will continue to increase, and the seats and floor mats will be full of dust mites. The closed interior during the humid rainy season is an ideal environment for mold to grow, so on sunny days, open all the doors to air it out. Throw away trash and spray disinfectant to dramatically improve the environment. Polishing the body is important, but keeping the interior equally glean essential.

Airing out clothes for about three hours is sufficient. From May to July, it's a good idea to keep about three dehumidifying packs in your car. Hanging up insect-repellent hooks for clothing is also effective.
